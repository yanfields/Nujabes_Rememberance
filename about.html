Seba Jun was born in the Nishi-Azabu distrcit of Minato, Tokyo. Jun was a Japanese hip-hop producer and DJ who recorded under the name Nujabes which is an anagram of his name.

Jun was the owner of Shibuya record stores, T Records and Guinness Records, and founder of the independent label Hydeout Productions. His style mixed hip-hop, rap, and jazz samples.

He was well known for his innate ability to combine his beats and rhythms into a mellow, nostalgic, and atmospheric sound. His work is well known throughout the hip-hop world and has taken part in numerous collaborations with other artists.

Jun was also a major contributor to the soundtrack of Samurai Champloo, and anime which blended feudal Japan with modern influences.

Jun passed away tragically when he was involved in a car accident on February 26, 2010.

However, because of the influence of his music and the man that he was, his memory spanwed numerous dedications and tributes from musicians around the world. This site is dedicated in rememberance to that memory and the music that he delivered to the world that has touched millions.
